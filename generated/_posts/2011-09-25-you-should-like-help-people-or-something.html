Nomad Travel Clinic

So yeah, I got my final injections today, and man are these things expensive. I totally recommend everyone to go get the ones you can on the NHS through your doctor instead of leaving it till the last minute like I have (my own personal doctor is back home in Doncaster and I've a feeling trying to get registered here in London will be like looking for a taxi to take you to Basecamp).

I was surprised such a little travel clinic existed in the center of London (it's not even on the outskirts near airports or anything - the one I went to was in Turnpike Lane ...) and apparently the owner of this chain (they've a few stores around London now) used to live around this area, which does kinda make sense I guess.

The branch I'd been frequenting was not a huge place, but big enough that each time I went, I was seen to by a different nurse - each being lovely in their own way (as nurses generally are, I guess). As today's nurse was preparing to stab me with bits of metal and chemical, I asked her about her work, to like, distract me from the impending pain (yeah, I'm a wuss).

Turns out she was freelance, and worked at Nomad whenever they needed her, but mostly she was a volunteer with VSO, and had only recently returned to London from a six month stint in Cameroon where she had set up her own clinic out in the sticks.

She spent her time helping village people who had no access to education or medical treatment and in return, they gave her undying gratitude - a far cry from what doctors and nurses receive in this country. I think she said Kenya might be next for her, and I can't help but be completely amazed at what this woman is accomplishing. The stories she told me in the 40 minutes I was there were incredible. She told me how she was treated like royalty, and how everyone in the village knew and greeted her. I doubt she was paid for her work, but they let her stay in a luxury apartment for free and offered to clean and cook for her. Makes me wonder why she bothers coming back to London.

Her job is to help countless people around the world for nothing.

My job is to help a multinational company sell tickets for music events so shareholders get more money.
